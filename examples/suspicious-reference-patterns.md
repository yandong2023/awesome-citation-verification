# Suspicious Reference Patterns

These patterns do not prove a citation is fake, but they should trigger review.

## DOI and title do not match

The DOI resolves, but the title belongs to a different paper.

Action: mark as `metadata mismatch` and replace the reference.

## Journal details look real but page range is impossible

The journal and year exist, but the volume, issue, or pages do not match the article.

Action: verify against publisher metadata or Crossref.

## Citation appears only in AI-generated text

The exact title has no search results in Google Scholar, PubMed, Crossref, OpenAlex, or publisher sites.

Action: mark as `not found`.

## Claim is stronger than the paper

The cited article reports an association, but the draft claims causation.

Action: rewrite the claim or find stronger evidence.

## Review cited as if it were a primary study

The paper is real, but the cited statement needs the original trial, cohort study, or experiment.

Action: trace the review's source and cite the primary paper when appropriate.

## Authors and year copied from one paper, title copied from another

This often happens when reference managers, AI tools, or manual edits merge metadata.

Action: verify each field separately and rebuild the citation from the database record.
